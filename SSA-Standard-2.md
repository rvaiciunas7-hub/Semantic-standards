# SSA Standard 2 — Structural Standard

The Structural Standard defines the required architectural structure for all materials produced within the Semantic Standards Authority (SSA).  
It establishes the mandatory form, modular relationships, and structural constraints that ensure institutional coherence.

## 1. Purpose
To define the structural architecture that all SSA documents, modules, and authoritative artifacts must follow.

## 2. Authority
This standard derives its authority from the Master Authoritative Artifact (MAA) located in `ssa-root`.  
All structural rules must align with MAA and operate within the semantic jurisdiction defined by SSTM.

## 3. Structural Principles
All SSA materials must adhere to the following principles:
- modularity  
- minimalism  
- semantic clarity  
- non‑redundancy  
- single‑origin authorship  
- alignment with institutional architecture  

## 4. Required Structure
Each SSA module must include:
- a root README defining its purpose  
- a reference to MAA  
- a clear boundary of scope  
- a minimal set of authoritative documents  
- no decorative or interpretive content  

## 5. Modular Relationships
Modules must:
- remain independent but interoperable  
- reference MAA as the authoritative source  
- avoid overlapping jurisdiction  
- maintain clear semantic boundaries  
- contribute to the overall SSA architecture  

## 6. Document Structure
All SSA documents must include:
- a title  
- a purpose section  
- an authority reference  
- a scope definition  
- normative content  
- integrity constraints  

## 7. Integrity Constraints
Structural integrity must be preserved by:
- avoiding structural drift  
- preventing redundancy  
- maintaining semantic lineage  
- ensuring consistency across modules  

## 8. Authorship
All structural definitions are authored solely by Renatas Vaičiūnas.  
No derivative works may alter SSA structural architecture.

## 9. Status
SSA Standard 2 is a core normative standard.  
All SSA modules and documents must comply with this structural architecture.
