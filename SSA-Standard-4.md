# SSA Standard 4 — Governance Standard

The Governance Standard defines the institutional governance principles of the Semantic Standards Authority (SSA).  
It establishes the rules, boundaries, and operational logic that govern SSA as a single‑author institutional authority.

## 1. Purpose
To define the governance model, decision‑making principles, and institutional boundaries that ensure SSA operates with structural clarity and semantic integrity.

## 2. Authority
This standard derives its authority from the Master Authoritative Artifact (MAA) located in `ssa-root`.  
All governance rules must align with the semantic and structural constraints defined in Standards 1–3 and the jurisdiction defined by SSTM.

## 3. Governance Model
SSA operates under the following governance principles:
- single‑author authority  
- modular institutional architecture  
- semantic jurisdiction through SSTM  
- non‑derivative governance  
- minimal and stable decision‑making structures  

## 4. Decision‑Making
All decisions within SSA:
- are made solely by the author, Renatas Vaičiūnas  
- must align with MAA  
- must preserve semantic and structural integrity  
- must avoid interpretive expansion or derivative governance layers  

## 5. Institutional Boundaries
SSA governance must:
- remain within the scope defined by MAA  
- avoid external delegation of authority  
- maintain strict authorship control  
- preserve the independence of SSA modules  
- prevent overlapping or conflicting governance structures  

## 6. Governance Artifacts
Governance artifacts include:
- MAA  
- SSA standards  
- SSA protocols  
- SSTM  
These artifacts collectively define the institutional governance framework.

## 7. Integrity Constraints
Governance integrity must be preserved by:
- preventing governance drift  
- maintaining semantic lineage  
- ensuring structural coherence  
- avoiding redundancy or interpretive governance layers  

## 8. Authorship
All governance rules and decisions are authored solely by Renatas Vaičiūnas.  
No derivative works may alter SSA governance.

## 9. Status
SSA Standard 4 is the authoritative governance standard.  
All SSA modules and documents must comply with this governance model.
