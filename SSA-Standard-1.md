# SSA Standard 1 — Primary Standard

The Primary Standard defines the foundational criteria for what constitutes a standard within the Semantic Standards Authority (SSA).  
It establishes the normative baseline for all subsequent SSA standards.

## 1. Purpose
To define the essential characteristics, structural requirements, and semantic constraints that qualify a document as an SSA standard.

## 2. Authority
This standard derives its authority from the Master Authoritative Artifact (MAA) located in `ssa-root`.  
All SSA standards must align with MAA and operate within the semantic jurisdiction defined by SSTM.

## 3. Definition of a Standard
A standard within SSA is a normative, non‑derivative, semantically constrained document that:
- defines a rule, requirement, or structural principle  
- maintains semantic consistency with SSTM  
- preserves authorship integrity  
- contributes to SSA’s modular architecture  
- cannot contradict MAA  

## 4. Structural Requirements
Each SSA standard must include:
- a clear title  
- a defined purpose  
- an authority reference to MAA  
- a scope statement  
- normative requirements  
- integrity constraints  

## 5. Semantic Constraints
All standards must:
- use terminology defined in SSTM  
- avoid ambiguity  
- maintain institutional minimalism  
- preserve semantic lineage  
- avoid interpretive or derivative expansions  

## 6. Authorship
All SSA standards are authored solely by Renatas Vaičiūnas.  
No derivative works may modify or reinterpret SSA standards.

## 7. Status
SSA Standard 1 is the primary and foundational standard.  
All subsequent standards must reference and comply with this document.
