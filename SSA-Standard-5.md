# SSA Standard 5 — Protocol Standard

The Protocol Standard defines the requirements, structure, and authority of all procedural protocols within the Semantic Standards Authority (SSA).  
It establishes how protocols are created, maintained, and aligned with SSA’s institutional architecture.

## 1. Purpose
To define the normative rules governing the creation, structure, and operation of SSA protocols.

## 2. Authority
This standard derives its authority from the Master Authoritative Artifact (MAA) located in `ssa-root`.  
All protocols must align with SSA Standards 1–4 and operate within the semantic jurisdiction defined by SSTM.

## 3. Definition of a Protocol
A protocol within SSA is a procedural, non‑derivative document that:
- defines an operational sequence or institutional procedure  
- maintains semantic and structural consistency  
- supports SSA governance and modular architecture  
- cannot contradict MAA or SSA standards  

## 4. Structural Requirements
Each SSA protocol must include:
- a title  
- a purpose statement  
- an authority reference to MAA  
- a procedural sequence  
- integrity constraints  
- a defined scope of application  

## 5. Procedural Principles
All SSA protocols must:
- follow minimal and clear procedural steps  
- avoid redundancy  
- maintain semantic clarity  
- preserve authorship integrity  
- support institutional coherence  

## 6. Protocol Boundaries
Protocols must not:
- redefine standards  
- introduce new semantic structures  
- alter governance rules  
- create derivative procedural layers  

## 7. Integrity Constraints
Protocol integrity must be preserved by:
- preventing procedural drift  
- maintaining alignment with MAA  
- ensuring consistency across modules  
- avoiding interpretive or ambiguous steps  

## 8. Authorship
All SSA protocols are authored solely by Renatas Vaičiūnas.  
No derivative works may modify or reinterpret SSA protocols.

## 9. Status
SSA Standard 5 defines the authoritative framework for all SSA protocols.  
All protocols must comply with this standard.
